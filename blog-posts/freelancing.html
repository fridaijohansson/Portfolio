What does it mean to be a freelancer?

Freelancing is a form of self-employment where the individual is the sole responsible for the work they produce. A freelancer can do commissioned work for clients or contracted work as part of a larger project in collaboration with studios and companies. Effectively, being a freelancer, you are the boss of yourself which means that the work, the hours you work, and value of that work, are up to you. Along with producing work, a freelancer within the creative industries needs to allocate time to develop and improve new skills to further their career and stay up to date with the technology in the industry.

There are a few key skills and tools which are necessary to be a well-rounded freelancer:

  • Being organised and effectively manage your time spent on working, learning, and marketing yourself.
  • You are providing a service, so being communicative, flexible, and quick thinking to solve any issues that may arise for the client or your work. This will leave a good impression which boosts your reputation within the industry allowing for more work in the future
  • Along with these skills, being a great team member is important but even more so when your work is limited within a project. You want to bring your best foot forward by being attentive and understanding when working with new people.
